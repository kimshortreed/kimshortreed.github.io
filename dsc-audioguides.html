What is a GPS actvated audio guide?

An audio guide is something you listen to in order to learn more about where you are, or what you are looking at.

Audio guides have been in common use in museums, art galleries, and walking tours for many years. In the before times, audio guides were on cassette tapes. But, modern, downloadable versions are in common use in places like The Metropolitan Museum of Modern Art (The Met), which has an extensive library of audi guides.

A GPS activated audio guide is an audio guide paired with GPS technology.

Using a digital map that integrates GPS coordinates, we can create an audio guide that activates when listeners are at a specific location anywhere on Earth, provided that spot has wifi or cellular data access.

This workshop uses izi.TRAVEL, "an open and free story-telling platform," to create a GPS activated audio guide.

Why make a custom audio guide to share your work?

  • Humanizes scholarship and learning
  • Provides and alternatives to textual or image content alone
  • Encourages knowledge to inhabit non-academic places and spaces
  • Allows you to get creative and innovative

Learning and outcomes

By the end of this workshop, you will learn how to...

  • create an izi.TRAVEL account
  • choose and edit a basic izi.TRAVEL outdoor tour template
  • upload an audio file (provided for you) to izi.TRAVEL's content management system
  • use izi.TRAVEL's map/GPS interface to place an audio event marker on a digital map
  • test and edit your audio tour's functionality
