What is a GPS activated audio guide?

An audio guide is something you listen to in order to learn more about where you are, or what you are looking at.

Audio guides have been in common use in museums, art galleries, and walking tours for many years. In the before times, audio guides were on cassette tapes. Modern, downloadable audio tours are in common use in places like The Met, which has an extensive library of audio guides.

A GPS activated audio guide is an audio guide paired with GPS technology.

Using a digital map that integrates GPS coordinates, we can create an audio guide that activates when listeners are at a specific location anywhere on Earth, provided that spot has wifi or cellular data access.

What is izi.TRAVEL and why use it?

This workshop uses izi.TRAVEL, "an open and free story-telling platform." The izi.TRAVEL platform is free and relatively simple to use, compared to similar platforms, and it has a relatively rich feature set.

izi.TRAVEL includes additional audio guide features, like images, videos, text content, and more, which you can explore, later. For now, we will create and test a barebones GPS activated audio guide tour, just to see how this aspect of the creation process works.

Why make a custom audio guide to share your work?

  • Humanizes scholarship and learning
  • Provides and alternatives to textual or image content alone
  • Encourages knowledge to inhabit non-academic places and spaces
  • Allows you to get creative and innovative

Hands-on activities

This self-paced section of the workshop walks you through the steps to create a basic GPS activated audio guide using the izi.TRAVEL platform.

Please work through the steps in the order and read each step carefully. If you are completing this workshop in person, then please let your facilitator know if you need a hand.

Now, let's get started 😸...


Step 1: open a browser and login to izi.TRAVEL

IMPORTANT: if you are using a browser on a phone, you will see a popup warning about using "the desktop version of the izi.TRAVEL CMS." Click the "Got it" button to continue.

Clink on this link to the "Creator" section of the izi.TRAVEL website: https://cms.izi.travel/users/sign_in?content_provider_id=93835

Step 2: In the izi.TRAVEL Dashboard page, click on the Create button


Step 3: Select "Tour"


Step 4: Name your Tour "UVic Fountain Tour" (without quotation marks) and click the Create button


Step 5: Upload your audio recording

IMPORTANT: first, download this audio file to your computer or phone: https://kimshortreed.github.io/UViFountainTourTest.m4a

TIPS: to download the audio file (from the above link) on a desktop or laptop, right click and choose "Save link as." On an iPhone, long press and select "Download Link." If you are saving to your phone, save it to your local (phone) storage and not a cloud storage service, like iCould.

Next, click on the Choose file button to upload the audio file you just saved to your computer.

Once your file is uploaded, you should see it appear below the Description text field.

IMPORTANT: remember to click on the Save button.


Step 6: Click on the Tour link

Clicking on the Tour link takes you to the map interface.


Step 7: Using the map interface, locate UVic and zoom in on the fountain

TIP: switch the map style to Satellite. This will make the fountain easier to find.

Nearly there! Now that we are happy with our general location, it is time to choose the spot where we want our tour's audio to play.


Step 8: Add and name a Tourist Attraction pin to the map

First, click on the Tourist Attraction pin icon. Note that your cursor will change to a plus sign.

Next, click on the map in the place you want your Tourist Attraction pin to be—clicking on the map will result in a popup window in which you name your Attraction.

Next, name your Attraction as follows: UVic Fountain

IMPORTANT: click on the Save button.

Next, scale the red boundary area around your pin so that it is (roughly) only around the fountain.

IMPORTANT: click on the Save button.

TIP: the GPS boundary area, pin, and Attraction icon become blue in colour to confirm that your Attraction changes have been saved.


Step 9: Return to the Properties area of your tour and navigate to the QR-Code tab

First, in the map interface, click on the gear icon to return to the Properties area.

We are already familiar with the Properties area's "Profile" tab, because this is where we named our tour in Step 4.

Next, click on the QR-Code tab.


Step 10: Use the QR code to activate your tour

Desktop or laptop QR code tips

For those on desktops or laptops, use your phone's camera app, as you would for any QR code, and point it at the QR code on your computer's screen. Doing so will automatically open the izi.TRAVEL phone app on your phone.

Phone QR code tips

If you are on an iPhone based browser (Safari), long press on the QR code from within the izi.TRAVEL CMS and choose "Open in izi.TRAVEL." Doing so will automatically open the izi.TRAVEL phone app on your phone.

Step 11: View your tour on your phone

If your tour opens as expected in the izi.TRAVEL phone app, click on the View Tour button.

TIP: if your tour does not display in the app, check out this izi.TRAVEL FAQ page for troubleshooting tips.

NOTE: you may see the following popup, which asks you to choose between downloading the tour to your phone, or using your Internet connection. The choice is yours...

Finally, when you are ready to try your tour outside, head toward the UVic fountain and click on the Start Tour button!

IMPORTANT: before you test your tour, make sure that you have enabled location services on your iPhone or Android phone.
